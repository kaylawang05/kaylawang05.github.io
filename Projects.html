Projects

I started “coding” in fifth grade after my parents introduced me to a Codecademy course on Python. I initially liked it because I could create cool projects, such as simple adventure games. But at the time, it was nothing more than that, just a way to make things that were interesting to me but relatively insignificant. All of that changed in 2019 when I saw Mark Rober’s “Using Drones to Plant 20,000,000 Trees.” As someone who was always conscious of the environment, it showed me how technology and engineering can be used to tackle issues I’m passionate about. Since then I’ve worked on projects with the goal of making a positive impact in my community.

Springfield Senior Housing Sign-in System

I developed a sign-in system for my grandparents' senior housing home to replace their paper system. During the COVID-19 pandemic, the digital records greatly improved the efficiency of contact tracing.

GitHub

Page by Page

Developed at the New Jersey Governor's School of Engineering and Technology, Page by Page is a browser extension that streamlines online learning. It's core function is a system that crowdsources online resources and recommends them based on quality and a multimedia learning model.

Paper

I presented the extension and research paper at the 2022 Governor's School Symposium with my project team members and submitted the paper to the MIT Undergraduate Research Technology Conference.

Focus Bank

Developed for a hackathon, this iOS app was inspired by an existing app that I frequently use called Forest. It sets a timer to keep you off your phone. To improve on it, my app plays ads while the timer is running to raise money for charity.
